After working as a lifeguard, and sitting through the pandemic working in marketing, I wanted to do something different that would take me away from my town and meet new people.

I came across a Coding Bootcamp with the University of Birmingham, and for 6 months I worked on websites, applications and engineering projects. I even got to present my projects infront of University professors.

I thoroughly enjoyed designing the applications much more than the building process. However, being able to turn my designs into real working apps is a skill I am very proud to have learnt.

One day, my tutor mentioned to me that he thought I would be good at UX Design ... What's that?... I went down a rabbit hole.

Designing products that contribute to society, researching the users I am designing for, learning about how to increase user retention & loyalty, emphathising with users to make their experience accessible and enjoyable... That sounds like a bit of me!

I am currently working at the BBC as a Junior Software Engineer, but I take every opportunity to learn and speak with current UX designers & get involved with UX projects. I am really hopeful that my future will head towards a career in UX design.
