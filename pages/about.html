After working as a lifeguard, and sitting through the pandemic working in marketing, I wanted to do something different that would take me away from my town and meet new people.

I came across a Coding Bootcamp, and for 6 months I worked on websites, applications and engineering projects. I worked in groups and met a lot of nice people, and even got to present my projects infront of University professors.

I thoroughly enjoyed designing the applications much more than the building process. However, being able to turn my designs into real working apps is a skill I am very proud to have learnt.

One day, my tutor said to me, 'Soph, I think you might like UX design' ... What's that?... I went down a rabbit hole.


Designing products that contribute to society, researching the users I am designing for, learning about how to increase user retention, emphathising with users to make their experience accessibile and enjoyable... That sounds like a bit of me!

I am currently working at the BBC as a Junior Software Engineer, but I take every opportunity to learn and speak with current UX designers to get involved with UX projects. I am really hopeful that my future will head towards a career in UX design.
