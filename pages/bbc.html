BBC GAMES

Games Social Profile

Designing & building components to celebrate achievements, find friends and personalise user experience on the BBC Games Index page

Overview

The BBC games index page has been developed to include a component that allows users to add their friends, view in-game achievements, and personalise their experience on the page by selecting a BBC branded character and corresponding colour scheme.

My role: Prototype designing, research and front-end development
Project: BBC Hackathon 22 and 23
Timeline: Two weeks
Tools: Figma, Miro, React

The Challenge

The BBC offers a brilliant bi-annual hackathon event where those who design and build our website get a chance to take a hold of the products they work on and create something exciting, new and beneficial to our users.
The theme of this challenge: Build Better Audience Communities. How could we advance our products and content formats to build better relationships with and between our users and audiences?
Over the course of two hackathons, the Games team has developed the Games Index page to offer a personalised and unique experience for its users.

Problem

Currently, the BBC Games page simply displays the free HTML5 games for children – there is no option for personalisation and no interaction between players, and therefore we rely solely on the games themselves for user retention.

Hypothesis

By creating a more personalised and unique experience on the BBC games index page, users will stay on the page for longer, discover more games and our audience size will increase.

Solution

Design and build various components that will sit in the BBC Games index page. One component will contain features that allow friends to interact, another will display the achievements the user has gained through game-play and the other will allow for the page to be personalised through the theming, colours and BBC branding that is so well known by our audience.

Research & Discovery

Competitor Analysis

Together, the team thought about which brands make games and make them well. What do they do that we could explore? We looked into big names such as Disney, Nintendo, PlayStation and Steam, and outlined their community features on a Miro board. We summarised that these brands offer a gaming profile that all overlap with similar features such as: profile image, username, ability to add friends, trophies and achievements and online status. These are the kind of features that we wanted to implement to our games page community component.

User Research

Here are some insights we took from previous testing on the Games Index page:

“Categorised content helps children make a choice by reducing the number of options they have to take in at a time."

1. Categorising games by popularity / what their friends are playing will help children make choices
2. This makes games easier to find

"They usually play a couple of games in one session."

Showing what games friends are playing / being able to favourite games will help with onward journeys.

Friction Points

Due to safeguarding and laws around storing information on children, we cannot allow users to sign-in or create an account. How might we get around this?

Username is generated automatically for the user, they can refresh this to something else but they would not be able to write their name and release any personal info. Everything will be saved locally, not put in a database.

By displaying achievements, this may create a ‘jealousy’ element amongst users, and may decrease the enjoyment a user has on the games page.
Any in-game achievements by the user can only be seen by that user. Achievement information would be stored locally, not in a database. Users would not be able to see their friends achievements.

The component will sit at the top of the page and this will interfere with the promo space that gets displayed when editorial have a new game that they want to show.
It is very uncommon for the promo space to get used. However, if the promo space does need to be used, the friends and achievements component will take a lower priority down the page and the promo space will remain in the usual position.
